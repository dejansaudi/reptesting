# MIT Design X (DHSX) - Complete Framework Mapping
## Comprehensive Venture Building Methodology

---

## OVERVIEW: The 8-Stage Process

The MIT Design X framework outlines a sequential venture building process with **8 primary stages**, flowing from problem discovery through market scaling:

1. **IDENTIFY** - Timing & Positioning Hypothesis
2. **DISCOVER** - Customer-Problem Fit
3. **DEFINE** - Problem-Solution Fit
4. **ENGAGE** - Validation & Product Launch
5. **CREATE** - Product-Market Fit
6. **DEVELOP** - Growth Optimization & Execution
7. **DEPLOY** - Scaling & Market Expansion
8. **SCALE** - Competitive Maturity & Market Leadership

---

## STAGE 0/1: TIMING & POSITIONING HYPOTHESIS
### Purpose: Market Entry Strategy & Opportunity Analysis

### Key Objectives:
- Find the right timing for market entry
- Identify innovators and early adopters
- Define the ideal customer vs. market segments
- Establish initial competitive positioning
- Create market mapping and dynamics analysis

### Frameworks & Tools:

**1. Market Problem Maps (Multiple)**
- Draw multiple problem maps to discover market opportunities
- Focus on IDEAL customer, never on generic segments
- Identify customer distribution patterns

**2. Barrier & Pain Level Matrix**
- **Barrier Level Scale:**
  - Entry not possible
  - Difficult to enter the segment
  - Normal entry but long sales cycles
  - Normal entry
  - Easy to enter the segment
- **Pain Level Scale (5 levels):**
  1. Has a problem but isn't aware of it
  2. Is aware of having a problem
  3. Been actively looking for a solution
  4. Assembled a solution out of parts
  5. Has/acquired a budget
- **Target: High Pain + High Barrier (Blue Ocean)**

**3. Market Stage Distribution Analysis**
- 2.5% = Innovators
- 13.5% = Early Adopters
- 34% = Early Majority
- 34% = Late Majority
- 16% = Laggards
- **Find innovators for initial market entry**

**4. Strategic Positioning Framework**
- Define 3+ competitive differentiations
- Map against problem-customer intersection
- Identify potential next segments after initial market

**5. TAM, SAM, SOM Definition**
- **TAM** (Total Addressable Market)
- **SAM** (Serviceable Available Market)
- **SOM** (Serviceable Obtainable Market)

**6. Value Proposition Architecture (5 Levels)**
- **Level 1: Core Value Proposition** - Fundamental reason for existence
- **Level 2: Unique Selling Proposition** - Differentiation claim
- **Level 3: Benefits** - What customer gains
- **Level 4: Features** - Product/service characteristics
- **Level 5: Problems** - Customer pain points addressed

**7. Initial Hypothesis Definition**
- State founding assumptions
- Define initial OKRs (Objectives & Key Results)
- Establish baseline KPIs

**8. Enablers & Market Positioning Through Time**
- Identify what enables market entry at this specific time
- Consider market readiness, technology maturity, regulatory environment

### Key Outputs:
- Market map with identified problems and segments
- Ideal customer profile (specific, not generic)
- Competitive differentiation thesis (3+ points)
- TAM/SAM/SOM quantification
- Initial OKRs and KPIs
- Market timing rationale

## STAGE 1: CUSTOMER-PROBLEM FIT
### Purpose: Validate Real Customer Problem and Build Deep Understanding

### Key Objectives:
- Understand the target segment deeply
- Validate that a significant problem exists
- Identify who is the ideal customer
- Map customer journey and pain points
- Confirm market dynamics support the opportunity
- Create detailed customer profiles

### Frameworks & Tools:

**1. Target Segment Definition**
- WHO is your ideal customer?
- Specific demographic, psychographic, behavioral characteristics
- Why this customer over others?
- Identify 1-3 ideal segments (not generic)

**2. Customer Profiling Suite**

**A. Persona Development**
- Name & Picture
- Title/Key Role & Organization
- Age & Gender
- Key Characteristics & Lifestyle
- **Problems the persona has** (that solution will solve)
- **Goals/Benefits** (what they want to achieve)
- **Journey Stories** (existing behaviors without solution)
- **Triggers/Emotions** (what initiates buying cycle)
- Think and Feel / See / Hear / Say and Do quadrants

**B. Empathy Map (4 Quadrants)**
- **THINK & FEEL:** What occupies their mind? Worries? Aspirations?
- **SEE:** What does the customer actually see? Appearance? Friends? Market?
- **HEAR:** What do they hear? From family? Friends? Media?
- **SAY & DO:** What behavior/words do they exhibit publicly?
- Plus: **PAIN** (obstacles & frustrations) & **GAIN** (desires & aspirations)

**C. User Journey Map**
- **5 Life Cycle Phases:**
  1. PRE-BUY: Awareness → Knowledge
  2. BUY: Consideration → Selection → Buying
  3. OWN: Satisfaction & Advocacy → Retention & Loyalty → End
- **Metrics per phase:**
  - Customer experience (positive/negative emotions)
  - Triggers
  - Verification activities
  - Epic tasks
- **Sales cycle alignment**

**D. Customer Journey Map (Extended)**
- Touch points across customer interaction
- Activities at each stage
- Emotional journey
- Pain points and opportunities
- Moment of truth identification

**E. Storyboard**
- Visual narrative of customer journey
- Problem identification
- Solution moment
- Happiness/success state

**F. Service Blueprint**
- Service phases and touch points
- Physical evidence
- Customer actions
- Front stage vs. back stage activities
- Support processes
- Time scale mapping

**3. Value Proposition Canvas**
Two-part framework:
- **LEFT SIDE (Customer Profile):**
  - Customer jobs (functional, emotional, social)
  - Pains (obstacles, frustrations, risks)
  - Gains (desired outcomes, aspirations)

- **RIGHT SIDE (Value Map):**
  - Products/Services offered
  - Pain relievers (how they address pains)
  - Gain creators (how they deliver gains)

**4. Market Dynamics Analysis**
- Stage of market maturity (growth, mature, decline)
- Competitive landscape positioning
- Market forces and drivers
- Timing considerations

**5. Strategy Canvas**
- Map how to differentiate from competition
- What to eliminate vs. enhance vs. create vs. reduce
- Competitive advantage visualization

**6. Competitive Position Mapping**
- Competitive differentiation 1, 2, 3
- Customer distribution analysis
- Ideal customer positioning vs. competitors

### Key Outputs:
- 1-3 detailed personas with journey maps
- Empathy map for each customer segment
- Complete user journey with touchpoints
- Service blueprint (if applicable)
- Value Proposition Canvas (customer-fit side)
- Market dynamics assessment
- Validation that problem exists and is valuable

**Assessment Gate:**
- ✓ Real customers with real problems found
- ✓ Problem pain level 4-5 (has budget or actively seeking)
- ✓ Barrier to entry acceptable (low-medium)
- ✓ Customer willing to pay/change behavior
- ✓ Repeatable customer acquisition possible

## STAGE 2: PROBLEM-SOLUTION FIT
### Purpose: Rapid Prototyping & Solution Validation

This stage focuses on developing low-cost solutions quickly to validate problem understanding.

### Key Objectives:
- Test multiple solution approaches
- Validate that customers want to buy/use solution
- Identify core features vs. nice-to-haves
- Build minimally viable solutions rapidly
- Establish product-market hypothesis

### Frameworks & Tools:

**1. Prototyping Methodologies (Less is More)**

**Whiteboard Prototyping**
- Sketch interfaces, flows, interactions
- Rapid iteration with customer feedback
- Cost: minimal, Speed: days

**Dummy Prototyping**
- Mockups using templates, wireframes
- Clickable prototypes (Figma, InVision)
- Cost: low, Speed: days-weeks

**Business Hypothesis (BH) Prototypes**
- Working demo focusing on core value proposition
- Smoke tests (landing pages, demand validation)
- Cost: low-medium, Speed: weeks

**MVP (Minimum Viable Product)**
- Smallest product that delivers core value
- 2-3 critical features only
- Sufficient to learn from real users
- Cost: medium, Speed: weeks-months

**2. Product Dynamics - The Hook Model**
- **TRIGGER:** What prompts user to take action?
  - Alarms, calls-to-action, emails, stores, authority
  - Or routines, situations, places, people, emotions
  - Transient vs. Habitual

- **ACTION:** What does user do?
  - Simplest action that leads to reward
  - Motivated by desire to solve pain or gain pleasure

- **VARIABLE REWARD:** What's the outcome?
  - Unpredictability increases engagement
  - Rewards of tribe, hunt, or self (psychological)

- **INVESTMENT:** What does user contribute?
  - Time, money, social capital, reputation
  - Creates switching cost and habit formation

**3. Storyboard for Solution**
- Visual narrative of: Problem → Solution → Happiness
- Customer interaction with product
- Emotional arc

**4. Develop Core Value Proposition**
- Refine from Stage 0-1 hypothesis
- Test messaging with customers
- Validate willingness to pay

**5. Customer Problem Fit Validation**
- Iterate until customer confirms problem exists
- Problem worth solving metric
- Customer satisfaction with problem framing

### Prototyping Best Practices (From Materials):
- Spend 80/20 on learning vs. building
- Test with real customers early
- Kill features that don't drive core value
- Iterate based on feedback
- Focus on must-have vs. nice-to-have

### Key Outputs:
- 2-3 prototype iterations
- Customer feedback summary
- Validated core features (MVP feature set)
- Product hypothesis statement
- Pricing/monetization hypothesis
- Go/No-Go decision for Stage 3

**Assessment Gate:**
- ✓ Core feature set identified
- ✓ Customers willing to try/buy product
- ✓ Solution addresses stated problem
- ✓ Repeatable delivery possible
- ✓ Pricing hypothesis validated

## STAGE 3: ENGAGEMENT & VALIDATION
### Purpose: Product Launch & Initial Market Validation

### Key Objectives:
- Launch MVP to target market
- Validate product-market fit indicators
- Build initial customer base
- Establish repeatable sales process
- Test go-to-market strategy
- Create financial model validation

### Frameworks & Tools:

**1. Business Model Canvas (Complete)**

**Core Elements:**
- **VALUE PROPOSITIONS:** What problems solved? What gains delivered?
  - V1: Core Value Proposition
  - V2-V6: Secondary value propositions

- **CUSTOMER SEGMENTS:**
  - Who are target customers?
  - Which segments most valuable?

- **CHANNELS:**
  - How do customers discover product?
  - How do they buy?
  - How do they get support?
  - Multi-channel mapping (earned media, paid media, direct sales, partners)

- **CUSTOMER RELATIONSHIP:**
  - How to acquire customers?
  - How to retain?
  - How to grow (cross-sell, upsell, next-sell)?
  - "Get, Keep, Grow" customer framework

- **REVENUE STREAMS:**
  - What are customers willing to pay?
  - How are they charged? (recurring, one-time, freemium, subscription)
  - What's the financial model?

- **KEY RESOURCES:**
  - What assets/resources needed?
  - Core vs. Strategic vs. Tactical resources
  - Human, physical, intellectual, financial

- **KEY ACTIVITIES:**
  - What must company do to deliver value?
  - Discovery, Validation, Efficiency, Scale phases
  - Production, Problem solving, Platform/Network

- **KEY PARTNERSHIPS:**
  - Who helps deliver value?
  - Distribution partners, technology partners, suppliers
  - Suppliers, channels, technical infrastructure

- **COST STRUCTURE:**
  - What are main costs?
  - Fixed vs. variable costs
  - Economics of scale

**2. Financial Cohort Analysis**
- **Cohort Definition:** Group of customers acquired in same period
- **Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR)** tracking per cohort
- **Lifetime Value (LTV)** calculation:
  - MRR × Months of retention
- **Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC):**
  - Sales + Marketing spend / new customers
- **LTV:CAC Ratio** (target 3:1 or better)
- **Payback Period** (months to recover CAC)
- **Churn Rate** (monthly and annual)

**3. Product-Market Fit Template**
- **Definition:** Product delivers such strong value that customers actively want it
- **Indicators:**
  - 40%+ of users say product is must-have
  - 50%+ month-over-month retention
  - Positive LTV:CAC ratio (3:1+)
  - Organic growth/referrals
  - Customer willingness to recommend (NPS)

**4. Get, Keep, Grow Customer Framework**

**GET CUSTOMERS (Acquisition):**
- Earned & Paid Media
- Referrals
- Contests & Events
- Blogs, RSS, Emails
- Direct Sales

**KEEP CUSTOMERS (Retention):**
- Product Updates
- Outreach Programs
- Loyalty Programs
- Customer Service

**GROW CUSTOMERS (Expansion):**
- Viral Loop
- Up-sell
- Cross-sell
- Next-sell

**5. Multi-Channel Map**
- Channel #1, #2, #3, #4...
- Conversion rates per channel
- Customer acquisition cost per channel
- Lifetime value per channel
- Multi-touch attribution (MCF)
- Channel grouping paths

**6. Validation Experiments**
- Falsifiable hypotheses (Specific Action → Expected Measurable Outcome)
- Minimum build plan to test hypothesis
- Measured results
- Learning (validated or invalidated)
- Next action/experiment

### Key Outputs:
- Complete Business Model Canvas (filled)
- Financial cohort analysis (6+ months data)
- Product-Market Fit validation
- Customer acquisition playbook
- Go-to-market strategy results
- Repeatable sales process

**Assessment Gate:**
- ✓ Product-Market Fit indicators met (40%+ must-have)
- ✓ Unit economics positive (LTV:CAC > 3:1)
- ✓ Repeatable customer acquisition process
- ✓ Retention metrics acceptable (50%+ MoM)
- ✓ Ready to scale operations

## STAGE 4: CREATE - PRODUCT-MARKET FIT
### Purpose: Optimize Product & Market Fit

### Key Objectives:
- Deepen product-market fit
- Optimize feature set based on usage data
- Refine messaging and positioning
- Increase unit economics
- Build competitive advantage
- Prepare for scaling

### Frameworks & Tools:

**1. Product-Market Fit Evolution**

From Stage 3's validation, now:
- **Reason to Come:** What brings new customers?
  - Strong value proposition
  - Word of mouth
  - Referral program

- **Reason to Stay:** What keeps customers?
  - Switching costs (data, integrations, workflows)
  - Network effects
  - Community

**2. Market Opportunity Analysis**

**Market Map Movement:**
- How is competitive landscape shifting?
- How are customer needs evolving?
- Where are blue ocean opportunities?
- Market maturity stage assessment

**3. Competitive Differentiation Analysis**

**Porter's Five Forces:**
- Threat of new entrants
- Bargaining power of suppliers
- Bargaining power of customers
- Threat of substitutes
- Rivalry among competitors

**Side-by-Side Competitive Analysis:**
- Feature comparison matrix
- Pricing comparison
- Customer service comparison
- Brand positioning comparison

**Balancing Act Analysis:**
- What to do vs. what competitors do
- Where to differentiate
- Where to meet parity
- Where to exceed

**4. Growth Optimization Framework**

**Channel Optimization:**
- Which channels deliver best ROI?
- Which channels drive best LTV customers?
- How to allocate marketing budget?
- Multi-touch attribution analysis

**Conversion Funnel Optimization:**
- Acquisition → Activation → Retention → Revenue → Referral (Pirate Metrics)
- Identify bottlenecks at each stage
- Test improvements (A/B testing)
- Measure impact on OMTM (One Metric That Matters)

**5. Stage 6 - Growth Optimization Execution**

Core Focus Areas:
- **WHO:** Target customer segments and channels
- **WHAT:** Value propositions for each segment
- **HOW:** Key activities and channels

For Each Channel:
- Activities
- Projects
- Conversion rates (%)
- Multi-channel funnel analysis
- Path conversion value (€/$)

### Key Outputs:
- Deepened competitive positioning
- Optimized customer acquisition channels
- Growth playbooks for each segment
- Operational efficiency improvements
- Readiness for scaling

## STAGE 5: DEVELOP - GROWTH OPTIMIZATION & EXECUTION
### Purpose: Scale Operations While Maintaining Growth Efficiency

### Key Objectives:
- Scale customer acquisition profitably
- Optimize operations for growth
- Build team and organizational capability
- Establish scalable processes
- Maintain unit economics at scale

### Frameworks & Tools:

**1. Nail It, Then Scale It Framework**

**FIND ENTRY (Nail It):**
- Customer Discovery phase
- Find ideal customer for problem at this market timing
- Build Product-Market Fit
- Validate repeatable acquisition model
- Establish base metrics

**EFFICIENT EXECUTION:**
- Customer Validation phase
- Scale Company phase
- Operational efficiency focus
- Process standardization

**SCALE IT:**
- Execute at scale
- Maintain unit economics
- Expand customer segments
- New product lines (if applicable)

**Metrics by Phase:**
- MRR (Monthly Recurring Revenue)
- Number of employees
- Scale-up model (year quarters)

**2. Customer Development Methodology**

**Phase 1: Customer Discovery**
- Get out of building
- Talk to 50+ customers
- Understand problems deeply
- Test hypotheses

**Phase 2: Customer Validation**
- Test business model
- Validate repeatable sales process
- Pivot or proceed
- Launch

**Phase 3: Customer Creation**
- Scale sales and marketing
- Create market demand
- Build awareness
- Execute go-to-market

**Phase 4: Company Building**
- Transition to operations-focused
- Build management structure
- Create scalable processes
- Develop corporate culture

**3. Experiment Plan/Report Framework**

For each growth initiative:
- **Background:** What are we trying to learn/achieve?
- **Falsifiable Hypothesis:** Specific Action → Expected Measurable Outcome
- **Experiment Scope:** Duration, sample size
- **Minimum Build Plan:** Step-by-step to get data
- **Results:** Enter measured data
- **Validated Learning:** Summary of what we learned
- **Validated or Invalidated:** Mark outcome
- **Next Action:** What experiment is next?

**4. One Metric That Matters (OMTM)**

Methodology:
1. **Ideate:** Generate idea backlog
2. **Analyze:** Assess hard + soft data
3. **Rank:** Prioritization framework (P.I.E.S. or B.R.A.S.S.)
4. **Execute:** Get it done!
   - Cross-skilled teams
   - Agile/Scrum methodology
   - Design & Test cycle
   - Minimum Viable Experiment
5. **Systemize/Productize:** Embed winning approach

**OMTM Selection:**
- Copy what others do + innovate
- Focus on ONE metric
- Combine hard data + soft insights
- Time-boxed experiments

**5. Lean Goals & Milestones**
- What are critical milestones?
- What dates are non-negotiable?
- What dependencies exist?
- What's the learning path?

**6. HR & Team Development**
- Hiring plan aligned with growth
- Team composition by phase
- Key roles and responsibilities
- Organizational structure evolution

### Key Outputs:
- Scalable operational processes
- Expanded team with capability
- Validated growth model at scale
- Customer acquisition cost optimization
- Readiness for Stage 6

**3. Peter Thiel's 7 Principles**

1. **ENGINEERING:** Do things hard but possible (10x better)
2. **TIMING:** Every moment in business happens once (market readiness)
3. **MONOPOLY:** Start small and monopolize (niche first)
4. **PEOPLE:** Nobody can do it alone (team capability)
5. **DISTRIBUTION:** Technology × Globalization (access/channels)
6. **DURABILITY:** 10-20 year vision (sustainable moat)
7. **SECRET:** The unbelievable truth (unfair advantage)

**Scoring Framework:**
- Engineering: What will you add AI/advanced tech to?
- Timing: Identify key trends enabling this moment
- Monopoly: How to create network effects or switching costs?
- People: Who will join the team? Why?
- Distribution: What's the distribution advantage?
- Durability: What endures vs. what fades?
- Secret: What do you know that others don't?
- **Target Score: 5.5+ out of 7**

## ASSESSMENT GATES BETWEEN STAGES

### Stage 0→1: Timing & Positioning → Customer Problem Fit
- ✓ Market timing validated
- ✓ Ideal customer identified
- ✓ Problem significance confirmed
- ✓ Barrier to entry acceptable
- ✓ Team understanding clear

### Stage 1→2: Customer Problem Fit → Problem Solution Fit
- ✓ Real customer validated
- ✓ Pain level 4-5 (has budget)
- ✓ Customer willing to change behavior
- ✓ Repeatable acquisition possible
- ✓ Ready to test solutions

### Stage 2→3: Problem Solution Fit → Engagement
- ✓ Core features identified
- ✓ MVP feature set clear
- ✓ Customers willing to buy/use
- ✓ Solution repeatable
- ✓ Pricing hypothesis validated

### Stage 3→4: Engagement → Create (PMF)
- ✓ Product-Market Fit indicators met (40%+ must-have)
- ✓ Unit economics positive (LTV:CAC > 3:1)
- ✓ Repeatable sales process proven
- ✓ Retention metrics acceptable (50%+ MoM)
- ✓ Ready to scale operations

### Stage 4→5: Create → Develop (Growth)
- ✓ PMF deeply validated
- ✓ Multi-channel acquisition model proven
- ✓ Operational efficiency improving
- ✓ Team scaling capabilities evident
- ✓ Ready for rapid scaling

### Stage 5→6: Develop → Deploy (Market Leadership)
- ✓ Market leadership indicators evident
- ✓ Competitive moat building
- ✓ Operations at scale proven
- ✓ Profitability trajectory clear
- ✓ Ready for expansion

### Stage 6→7: Deploy → Scale (Maturity)
- ✓ Market share leadership achieved
- ✓ Organizational capability at scale
- ✓ Analytics infrastructure ready
- ✓ Culture and values embedded
- ✓ Ready for data-driven optimization

### Stage 7→8: Ongoing Optimization
- ✓ Analytics maturity (Descriptive → Diagnostic → Predictive → Prescriptive)
- ✓ Predictive models deployed
- ✓ Automated decision-making implemented
- ✓ Continuous optimization culture
- ✓ Sustained competitive advantage

## KEY TERMINOLOGY & CONCEPTS

**CAC** - Customer Acquisition Cost (marketing spend / new customers)

**LTV** - Lifetime Value (MRR × months of retention)

**LTV:CAC Ratio** - Target 3:1 (spend $1 to get $3 lifetime value)

**MRR** - Monthly Recurring Revenue

**OMTM** - One Metric That Matters (the single most important KPI)

**OKR** - Objectives & Key Results (goal-setting framework)

**PMF** - Product-Market Fit (product so valued customers actively want it)

**MVP** - Minimum Viable Product (simplest solution delivering core value)

**TAM/SAM/SOM** - Total/Serviceable/Serviceable Obtainable Market

**Pivot** - Change core aspect of business model

**Validated Learning** - Learning from experiment with real market data

**Cohort** - Group of customers acquired in same time period

**Churn** - Rate at which customers stop using product

**Retention** - Rate at which customers continue using product

**NPS** - Net Promoter Score (customer recommendation likelihood)

**Hypothesis-Driven Experimentation** - Test specific claims with measurable outcomes

## INTEGRATION NOTES

For merging with existing startup school frameworks:

1. **Linear Progression:** The 8 stages flow sequentially with clear gates between them
2. **Iterative Learning:** Each stage builds on previous validation, allowing pivots if needed
3. **Customer-Centric:** Every stage involves customer interaction and validation
4. **Data-Driven:** Evolution from hypothesis-driven to analytics-driven decision making
5. **Operational Maturity:** Progression from lean startup to scaled operations
6. **Framework Flexibility:** Can adapt frameworks (Canvas, Maps, etc.) to specific context
7. **Team Evolution:** Organization structure and size evolves with stage
8. **Metric Evolution:** KPIs shift from validation (% must-have) to operational (MRR, churn, CAC)

This structure enables systematic venture building from idea validation through market maturity, with clear decision points and proven tools at each stage.
