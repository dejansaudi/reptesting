THE 8-STAGE FLOW AT A GLANCE
================================================================================

Stage 0-1: IDENTIFY & DISCOVER
├─ Find market timing and positioning
├─ Validate customer and problem exist
├─ Define TAM/SAM/SOM
├─ Create customer personas and journey maps
└─ Success: Problem validated with high-pain customers (level 4-5)

Stage 2-3: DEFINE & ENGAGE
├─ Rapid prototype (whiteboard→dummy→MVP)
├─ Test solution with customers
├─ Identify core features
└─ Success: Customers willing to buy/use MVP

Stage 3-4: ENGAGEMENT & VALIDATION
├─ Launch MVP to market
├─ Build initial customer base
├─ Establish repeatable sales process
├─ Create business model canvas
├─ Calculate unit economics
└─ Success: PMF validated (40%+ must-have + LTV:CAC > 3:1)

Stage 4-5: CREATE & DEVELOP
├─ Deepen product-market fit
├─ Optimize product based on usage data
├─ Refine competitive positioning
├─ Multi-channel growth experimentation
├─ Build organizational capability
└─ Success: Multi-channel growth at scale

Stage 5-6: DEVELOP & DEPLOY
├─ Scale customer acquisition profitably
├─ Expand to adjacent markets/segments
├─ Build management structure
├─ Establish market leadership
└─ Success: Market share leadership

Stage 6-7: DEPLOY & SCALE
├─ Competitive differentiation deepening
├─ Operational excellence
├─ Analytics infrastructure
├─ Expand market presence
└─ Success: Competitive moat established

Stage 7-8: SCALE & MATURITY
├─ Predictive analytics deployed
├─ Automated decision-making
├─ Long-term vision execution
├─ Continuous optimization culture
└─ Success: Sustained competitive advantage

CRITICAL SUCCESS GATES (GO/NO-GO DECISIONS)
================================================================================

GATE 1→2: Do we have a real customer with a real problem?
✓ Customer pain level 4-5 (has budget or actively seeking solution)
✓ Customer willing to change existing behavior
✓ Repeatable customer acquisition possible
✓ Market timing appropriate

GATE 2→3: Does the solution deliver core value?
✓ MVP addresses customer's stated problem
✓ Customers willing to buy/try solution
✓ Core features identified (2-3 critical ones)
✓ Solution can be delivered repeatably

GATE 3→4: DO WE HAVE PRODUCT-MARKET FIT?
✓ 40%+ of users say product is "must-have"
✓ LTV:CAC ratio > 3:1 (unit economics positive)
✓ Repeatable sales/acquisition process proven
✓ 50%+ month-over-month customer retention
✓ Customers willing to recommend (high NPS)
→ This is THE critical gate. Most ventures fail to pass this.

GATE 4→5: Can we scale this profitably?
✓ Multi-channel acquisition model working
✓ Unit economics improve with scale
✓ Operational processes standardized
✓ Team capability for rapid growth

GATE 5→6: Are we becoming market leader?
✓ Market share leadership indicators
✓ Competitive differentiation clear
✓ Organizational capability at scale
✓ Profitability trajectory positive

GATE 6→7: Can we sustain competitive advantage?
✓ Operations sustainable at scale
✓ Analytics infrastructure ready
✓ Team culture and values embedded
✓ Market positioning defensible

GATE 7→8: Can we optimize and predict?
✓ Predictive models deployed and accurate
✓ Automated decision-making implemented
✓ Analytics driving 80%+ of decisions
✓ Competitive moat strong

KEY METRICS BY STAGE (WHAT TO MEASURE)
================================================================================

STAGES 0-1 (DISCOVERY):
- Problem significance (customer validation level)
- Pain level (1-5 scale: target 4-5)
- Barrier to entry (target: manageable)
- TAM/SAM/SOM quantification
- Customer willingness to pay (hypothesis)

STAGES 2-3 (DEFINITION):
- Prototype iteration speed
- Customer feedback sentiment
- Core feature identification
- Feature adoption rates
- Customer acquisition cost hypothesis

STAGES 3-4 (VALIDATION) ← CRITICAL MEASUREMENT POINT
- CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost) = Sales+Marketing $ / New Customers
- LTV (Lifetime Value) = MRR × Months Retained
- LTV:CAC Ratio (target: > 3:1) = unit economics positive
- Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR)
- Month-over-month retention (target: 50%+)
- % saying "must-have" (target: 40%+)
- NPS (Net Promoter Score)
- Payback period (target: < 12 months)
- Cohort analysis by acquisition channel

STAGES 4-5 (GROWTH):
- OMTM (One Metric That Matters) per sprint
- Multi-channel CAC comparison
- Channel mix optimization
- LTV improvement trajectory
- Operational leverage
- Conversion funnel metrics
- Customer acquisition growth rate (target: 10%+ MoM)

STAGES 5-6 (EXPANSION):
- Market share by segment
- Revenue per employee
- Team scaling efficiency
- Operational cost reduction
- Customer acquisition at scale
- Churn rate reduction
- Adjacent market penetration

STAGES 6-7 (LEADERSHIP):
- Competitive position ranking
- Market leadership indicators
- Brand awareness/preference
- Organizational capability maturity
- Employee retention
- Culture fit scores

STAGES 7-8 (ANALYTICS):
- Predictive model accuracy
- Automated decision success rate
- Customer lifetime value at scale
- Profitability per customer
- Optimization improvement %
- Time to insight reduction
- Decision-making speed improvement

================================================================================
THE PAIN-BARRIER MATRIX: FINDING BLUE OCEAN
================================================================================

PAIN LEVEL (How much does customer feel the pain?):
1. Has problem but isn't aware ← Hard to sell to
2. Is aware of problem ← Easier to sell to
3. Been actively looking for solution ← Good prospect
4. Assembled solution from other parts ← SWEET SPOT
5. Has/acquired a budget ← BEST TARGET

BARRIER TO ENTRY (How hard is it to win this segment?):
- Entry not possible ← Avoid
- Difficult to enter ← High risk
- Normal entry, long sales cycles ← Average
- Normal entry ← SWEET SPOT
- Easy to enter ← Competitive pressure

STRATEGY: Find intersections with HIGH PAIN (4-5) + MANAGEABLE BARRIER
These are "blue ocean" opportunities - valuable, accessible segments.

================================================================================
QUICK DIAGNOSTIC: WHERE ARE YOU?
================================================================================

Check your metrics:

If PMF not yet validated (< 40% must-have OR LTV:CAC < 3:1):
→ You're in STAGES 0-3 (DISCOVERY & DEFINITION)
→ Focus: Problem validation, prototype iteration, customer learning
→ Metrics that matter: Problem significance, customer feedback, CAC hypothesis
→ Question: Do customers actually want what we're building?

If PMF being validated (40%+ must-have AND LTV:CAC > 3:1):
→ You're in STAGE 4 (VALIDATION)
→ Focus: Scaling acquisition, refining product, proving repeatability
→ Metrics that matter: CAC, LTV, retention, unit economics
→ Question: Can we grow this profitably?

If PMF validated, growing 10%+ MoM, multi-channel working:
→ You're in STAGES 5-6 (GROWTH & EXPANSION)
→ Focus: Operational efficiency, team scaling, market expansion
→ Metrics that matter: OMTM per sprint, CAC improvement, LTV growth
→ Question: How do we scale without losing efficiency?

If market leader status, expanding adjacently, optimizing operations:
→ You're in STAGES 6-7 (LEADERSHIP & DEPLOYMENT)
→ Focus: Competitive moat, organizational excellence, market dominance
→ Metrics that matter: Market share, revenue per employee, profitability
→ Question: How do we maintain leadership?

If deploying predictive analytics, automating decisions, optimizing continuously:
→ You're in STAGE 8 (MATURITY & AGI-READY)
→ Focus: Analytics maturity (Descriptive → Diagnostic → Predictive → Prescriptive)
→ Metrics that matter: Model accuracy, automation efficiency, decision speed
→ Question: How do we optimize for long-term competitive advantage?

KEY FRAMEWORKS YOU'LL USE
================================================================================

PROFILING & CUSTOMER UNDERSTANDING:
- Personas (profile, problems, goals, triggers)
- Empathy Maps (think/feel, see, hear, say/do, pain, gain)
- User Journey Maps (pre-buy, buy, own phases with emotions)
- Storyboarding (problem → solution → happiness narrative)
- Service Blueprints (touchpoints, front/back stage, support)

MARKET & COMPETITIVE ANALYSIS:
- Market Maps (problems, competition, segments)
- Pain-Barrier Matrix (pain level 1-5, barrier level scaling)
- Strategy Canvas (factors to reduce/eliminate/raise/create)
- Competitive Position Mapping (differentiation points)
- Porter's Five Forces (threat analysis)

VALUE PROPOSITION:
- 5-Level Value Proposition (core → unique → benefits → features → problems)
- Value Proposition Canvas (customer profile vs. value map)

BUSINESS MODEL:
- Business Model Canvas (9 blocks: partners, activities, resources, value props, relationships, channels, segments, revenue, costs)
- Lean Canvas (simplified, startup-focused version)
- Financial Cohort Analysis (MRR, LTV, CAC per customer group)

GROWTH & OPTIMIZATION:
- Get/Keep/Grow Customer Framework (acquisition, retention, expansion)
- Multi-Channel Map (attribution, CAC, LTV by channel)
- OMTM Process (one metric that matters framework)
- Experiment Plan/Report (hypothesis → test → learning → next)
- Pirate Metrics (AARRR: Acquisition, Activation, Revenue, Retention, Referral)

ANALYTICS & DATA:
- Analytics Maturity Model (Descriptive → Diagnostic → Predictive → Prescriptive)
- Hypothesis Testing Stages (discovery, validation, optimization, alarm)
- From Noise to Signal (speed, focus, knowledge, decision support)

STRATEGIC:
- Peter Thiel 7 Factors (engineering, timing, monopoly, people, distribution, durability, secret)
- Long-Term Vision Framework (purpose, vision, mission, values, secret, motivation, skills, habits)
- Important vs. Urgent Matrix (prioritization framework)
