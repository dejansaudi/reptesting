MIT DESIGN X (DHSX) - QUICK REFERENCE GUIDE
8-Stage Venture Building Methodology - One Page Summary
================================================================================

THE 8 STAGES AT A GLANCE:

1. IDENTIFY              2. DISCOVER              3. DEFINE               4. ENGAGE
   Timing &                Customer-Problem         Problem-Solution        Validation &
   Positioning             Fit                      Fit                     Launch
   ↓                       ↓                        ↓                       ↓
   Find right timing       Validate real problem    Rapid prototype         MVP launch &
   market entry            exists                   2-3 iterations          initial growth

5. CREATE               6. DEVELOP               7. DEPLOY               8. SCALE
   Product-Market          Growth                   Market                  Analytics &
   Fit                     Optimization             Expansion               Maturity
   ↓                       ↓                        ↓                       ↓
   Deepen PMF &            Scale operations         Establish               Data-driven
   optimize                profitably               market leadership       optimization

================================================================================
CRITICAL SUCCESS GATES BETWEEN STAGES
================================================================================

Stage 1→2: Real customer with high pain level (4-5) + willing to change behavior
Stage 2→3: MVP delivers core value + customers willing to buy/use
Stage 3→4: PMF validated (40%+ must-have) + Unit economics positive (LTV:CAC 3:1+)
Stage 4→5: Multi-channel growth proven + Operational efficiency improving
Stage 5→6: Repeatable growth at scale + Market leadership indicators
Stage 6→7: Operations sustainable + Analytics infrastructure ready
Stage 7→8: Predictive models deployed + Continuous optimization culture

================================================================================
ESSENTIAL METRICS BY PHASE
================================================================================

DISCOVERY PHASE:
- Problem significance (pain level 4-5 on scale)
- Customer willingness to change behavior
- Repeatable customer acquisition possible

VALIDATION PHASE (THE KEY GATES):
- 40%+ saying product is "must-have" (PMF indicator)
- LTV:CAC Ratio > 3:1 (unit economics positive)
- CAC = Customer Acquisition Cost
- LTV = Lifetime Value = MRR × Months Retained
- Payback period < 12 months
- Cohort retention 50%+ month-over-month

GROWTH PHASE:
- OMTM: One Metric That Matters identified
- Multi-channel CAC comparison
- Cohort LTV improvement trajectory
- Operational leverage improving

SCALE PHASE:
- MRR (Monthly Recurring Revenue) growth rate
- Market share leadership
- Employee productivity (revenue per employee)
- Profitability trajectory

================================================================================
CUSTOMER SEGMENTATION: THE PAIN-BARRIER MATRIX
================================================================================

PAIN LEVEL SCALE (Target 4-5):
1. Has problem but isn't aware
2. Is aware of having problem
3. Been actively looking for solution
4. Assembled solution from other parts ← SWEET SPOT
5. Has/acquired a budget ← BEST TARGET

BARRIER TO ENTRY SCALE:
- Entry not possible (avoid)
- Difficult to enter the segment
- Normal entry but long sales cycles
- Normal entry ← SWEET SPOT
- Easy to enter the segment ← BEST TARGET

STRATEGY: Find HIGH PAIN + LOW BARRIER combination (Blue Ocean)

================================================================================
THE 5-LEVEL VALUE PROPOSITION ARCHITECTURE
================================================================================

LEVEL 1: CORE VALUE PROPOSITION
         ↓ (Fundamental reason for existence?)
LEVEL 2: UNIQUE SELLING PROPOSITION
         ↓ (Specific differentiation?)
LEVEL 3: BENEFITS
         ↓ (What does customer gain?)
LEVEL 4: FEATURES
         ↓ (Product characteristics?)
LEVEL 5: PROBLEMS
         (Pain points addressed?)

================================================================================
OMTM (ONE METRIC THAT MATTERS) PROCESS
================================================================================

Step 1: IDEATE - Generate candidate metrics
Step 2: ANALYZE - Assess hard data + soft insights (copy others + go crazy)
Step 3: RANK - Prioritize by impact/effort (P.I.E.S. or B.R.A.S.S.)
Step 4: EXECUTE:
   a) State hypothesis: (Specific Action) → (Expected Measurable Outcome)
   b) Create minimum build plan
   c) Design & test cycle
   d) Time-box experiment (days, not months)
Step 5: SYSTEMIZE - Embed winning approach into standard operations

BUSINESS MODEL CANVAS: THE 9 BLOCKS
================================================================================

KEY PARTNERS    KEY ACTIVITIES    KEY RESOURCES     VALUE PROPOSITIONS    CUSTOMER
                                                                         SEGMENTS
                                   ↓ CHANNELS ↓
                                   ↓ RELATIONSHIPS ↓
                                   ↓ REVENUE STREAMS ↓
COST STRUCTURE  (Wrap: GET-KEEP-GROW customer strategy)

KEY ELEMENTS:
- VALUE PROPOSITIONS: V1-V6 benefits, problems solved
- CUSTOMER SEGMENTS: Who are target customers?
- CHANNELS: Sales, support, distribution, partnerships
- CUSTOMER RELATIONSHIPS: Get (acquire), Keep (retain), Grow (expand)
- REVENUE STREAMS: Subscription, one-time, freemium, etc.
- KEY RESOURCES: Team, capital, technology, partners
- KEY ACTIVITIES: What must company do?
- KEY PARTNERS: Suppliers, channels, strategic partners
- COST STRUCTURE: Fixed + variable costs, economies of scale

================================================================================
GET, KEEP, GROW CUSTOMER FRAMEWORK
================================================================================

GET CUSTOMERS (Acquisition):
- Earned & Paid Media (ads, PR, content)
- Referrals (word-of-mouth, affiliate)
- Contests & Events
- Blogs, RSS, Emails
- Direct Sales

KEEP CUSTOMERS (Retention):
- Product Updates & Improvements
- Outreach Programs
- Loyalty Programs
- Customer Service Excellence

GROW CUSTOMERS (Expansion Revenue):
- Viral Loops (invite friends)
- Up-sell (better tier)
- Cross-sell (related product)
- Next-sell (new products)

Formula: Customer LTV = (MRR × Months Retained) - (Total Get+Keep+Grow costs)

================================================================================
CUSTOMER JOURNEY MAP: THE 3 PHASES
================================================================================

PRE-BUY PHASE:
- Awareness → Knowledge
- Customer recognizes problem exists
- Starts searching for solutions

BUY PHASE:
- Consideration → Selection → Purchase Decision
- Customer evaluates options
- Makes final buying decision

OWN PHASE:
- Satisfaction & Advocacy → Retention & Loyalty → End/Renewal
- Customer uses product
- Becomes repeat buyer or advocate

Metrics at each phase: Customer experience (emotions), triggers, activities, pain points

================================================================================

    2.5%        13.5%       34%         34%         16%
  INNOVATORS  EARLY ADO   EARLY MAJ   LATE MAJ    LAGGARDS
   (Risk      (Vision)   (Pragmat)   (Conserv)   (Skeptic)

STRATEGY: Target innovators (2.5%) first, then early adopters (13.5%)
These are most likely to buy new solutions and give feedback.

================================================================================
PETER THIEL'S 7 SUCCESS FACTORS (Score 0-1, target 5.5+)
================================================================================

1. ENGINEERING - 10x better than alternative (hard but possible)
2. TIMING - Right moment in market history (every moment happens once)
3. MONOPOLY - Start small and dominate niche (network effects, switching costs)
4. PEOPLE - Exceptional team (nobody can do it alone)
5. DISTRIBUTION - Technology × Globalization (access to customers)
6. DURABILITY - 10-20 year vision (sustainable competitive moat)
7. SECRET - What do you know that competitors don't? (unfair advantage)

Assessment: What will you add AI/advanced tech to? Who will lose jobs? Key trends?

================================================================================
THE HOOK MODEL: BUILDING HABIT LOOPS
================================================================================

    TRIGGER
       ↓
    ACTION → VARIABLE REWARD → INVESTMENT
       ↑_______________________↑

TRIGGER: External (alert, email) or habitual (routine, emotion, place, person)
ACTION: Simplest behavior to get reward (low friction)
REWARD: Unpredictability increases engagement (variable, not guaranteed)
INVESTMENT: User invests time/data/reputation = switching cost + habit formation

Example: "Every time the user [trigger], they use [product feature]"

IMPORTANT vs. URGENT PRIORITIZATION MATRIX
================================================================================

              LOW URGENCY           HIGH URGENCY
HIGH IMPORTANCE
   SCHEDULE IT                         JUST DO IT
   (Exercise, Planning)                (Crisis, bugs)

LOW IMPORTANCE
   DON'T DO IT                     DELEGATE/PUSH BACK
   (Filing old docs)               (Others' deadlines)

Strategy: Focus time on HIGH IMPORTANCE tasks, regardless of urgency

KEY TERMINOLOGY & DEFINITIONS
================================================================================

CAC = Customer Acquisition Cost (Total sales & marketing / New customers)
LTV = Lifetime Value (MRR × Average months of retention)
MRR = Monthly Recurring Revenue (total predictable monthly revenue)
OMTM = One Metric That Matters (the single most important KPI right now)
OKR = Objectives & Key Results (goal-setting framework)
PMF = Product-Market Fit (product valued so customers actively want it)
MVP = Minimum Viable Product (simplest solution delivering core value)
TAM = Total Addressable Market (all potential customers)
SAM = Serviceable Available Market (addressable portion we can reach)
SOM = Serviceable Obtainable Market (realistic capture in near term)
NPS = Net Promoter Score (customer willingness to recommend 0-100)
Churn = Rate customers stop using product (monthly or annual)
Retention = Rate customers keep using product (opposite of churn)
Pivot = Fundamental change to business model based on learning
Validated Learning = Knowledge gained from market experiment
